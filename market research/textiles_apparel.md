# Market Sourcing Report: Textiles & Apparel
## 1. Supply-Side Analysis
### 1.1 Synthetic Yarn (Polyester, Nylon, Blended Yarns)
China (The Petrochemical & Synthetic Giant)
- Major Manufacturing Hubs:
- Xiaoshan & Shaoxing (Zhejiang Province): The largest polyester yarn and filament production cluster globally. Shaoxing (specifically Keqiao) is the epicenter of synthetic fabric manufacturing.
- Shengze & Suzhou (Jiangsu Province): Massive production base for polyester chips, filament yarns (DTY, FDY, POY), and high-tenacity industrial yarns.
- Changle (Fujian Province): Major hub for nylon filament yarn and warp-knitting synthetic yarns.
- Production Capacities:
China accounts for over 70% of global polyester filament yarn production. Major private conglomerates (e.g., Tongkun Group, Hengyi Petrochemical, and Shenghong Group) operate mega-refineries and spinning plants.
- Pricing Structure (FOB China):
- Polyester DTY (75D/36F): $1.05 - $1.25 USD/kg
- Polyester POY (150D/48F): $0.90 - $1.10 USD/kg
- Nylon 6 DTY (70D/24F): $1.90 - $2.30 USD/kg
Vietnam (The Rapidly Expanding Spinners)
- Major Manufacturing Hubs:
- Dong Nai & Binh Duong Provinces (Southern Vietnam): Key spinning zones hosting massive foreign direct investments (FDI) from Taiwan (Formosa Chemicals & Fiber), South Korea (Hyosung), and China (Texhong).
- Nam Dinh Province (Northern Vietnam): A traditional textile hub modernized with synthetic yarn spinning mills.
- Production Capacities:
Vietnam has a synthetic spinning capacity of around 800,000 to 1 million metric tons per annum. It remains dependent on importing raw polyester chips and PTA/MEG from China and Taiwan.
- Pricing Structure (FOB Vietnam):
Typically 5% to 8% higher than Chinese factory-direct pricing due to imported raw materials and higher electricity tariffs:
- Polyester DTY (75D/36F): $1.15 - $1.35 USD/kg
- Polyester POY (150D/48F): $0.98 - $1.18 USD/kg

### 1.2 Readymade Garments (RMG)
China (The Automated Fashion Factory)
- Major Manufacturing Hubs:
- Guangdong Province (Guangzhou, Dongguan, Shenzhen): Epicenter for fast-fashion apparel, casual wear, and intimate apparel.
- Zhejiang Province (Ningbo, Wenzhou, Shaoxing): Major hubs for formal menswear (suits, shirts), outerwear, and knitwear.
- Fujian Province (Quanzhou, Shishi): Centered around activewear, sportswear, and casual outerwear.
- Production Capacities:
Unrivaled global capacity. Chinese garment factories feature extreme automation (robotic fabric cutting, automated hanger assembly lines, and computer-guided sewing). China remains the world's largest apparel exporter, exporting over $140 billion annually.
- Pricing Structure (FOB China):
- Basic Crewneck T-Shirt (OEM): $1.50 - $2.50 USD
- Men's Woven Shirt (OEM): $4.00 - $7.00 USD
- Polyester Activewear Hoodies: $6.00 - $12.00 USD
- Flexible MOQs: Often starting at 500-1,000 pieces per style.
Vietnam (The Low-Cost Bulk Expert)
- Major Manufacturing Hubs:
- Red River Delta (Hanoi, Hai Phong, Nam Dinh): Specialized in heavy outerwear, winter jackets, and sportswear.
- Mekong Delta (Ho Chi Minh City, Binh Duong, Long An): Center for casual wear, denim, activewear, and knitwear.
- Production Capacities:
Vietnam is the world's third-largest exporter of garments, dominated by large State-Owned Enterprises (like Vinatex) and massive FDI plants from South Korea (Hansae, Sae-A), Taiwan, and China. Annual export capacity exceeds $40 billion.
- Pricing Structure (FOB Vietnam):
Highly competitive for long-run, high-volume orders. Labor costs are approximately 30-40% lower than in China's coastal manufacturing hubs:
- Basic Crewneck T-Shirt (OEM): $1.20 - $2.00 USD
- Men's Woven Shirt (OEM): $3.50 - $6.00 USD
- MOQs: Generally higher, typically 3,000+ pieces per style.

### 1.3 Industrial Fabrics (Warp-Knit, Geotextiles, Coated Tarpaulins)
China (The Technical Textiles King)
- Major Manufacturing Hubs:
- Haining (Zhejiang Province): The "Warp Knitting Capital of China", producing massive volumes of geotextiles, PVC-coated flex banners, tarpaulins, and automotive seating fabrics.
- Changzhou & Wuxi (Jiangsu Province): Specialized in high-strength industrial filter fabrics, glass fiber textiles, and geo-grids.
- Shandong Province (Qingdao, Dezhou): Major production base for non-woven agricultural fabrics and construction geotextiles.
- Production Capacities:
China accounts for over 50% of global technical and industrial textiles output, with Haining alone processing over 1.5 million tons of industrial fabrics annually.
- Pricing Structure (FOB China):
- PVC-Coated Tarpaulin (500 GSM): $0.85 - $1.30 USD/sqm
- Woven Geotextile (Polypropylene): $0.40 - $0.75 USD/sqm
Vietnam (The Growing Technical Base)
- Major Manufacturing Hubs:
- Dong Nai & Long An Provinces: Hosts foreign investments from Taiwan (Far Eastern New Century) and South Korea (Hyosung), specializing in tire cord fabrics and airbag materials.
- Production Capacities:
Limited to specific high-volume sub-sectors (such as polyester tire cords for the global automotive supply chain). Vietnam lacks a broad technical textile ecosystem and imports raw industrial yarns to weave or coat fabrics.
- Pricing Structure (FOB Vietnam):
Typically 5% to 10% higher than Chinese equivalents for standard geotextiles and coated fabrics due to intermediate yarn import costs:
- PVC-Coated Tarpaulin (500 GSM): $0.95 - $1.45 USD/sqm

## 2. Demand-Side Analysis (India, specifically Gujarat)
### 2.1 Import Volumes & Demand Drivers
- Surat (The Synthetic Capital of India): Processes over 40% of the nation's man-made fiber (MMF) textiles, housing over 600,000 power looms, 100,000 embroidery machines, and 450 processing houses. Surat consumes massive quantities of polyester filament yarn (PFY) and nylon yarn. Specialized micro-denier, high-stretch, recycled polyester yarns, and low-cost raw yarns are imported in large volumes to meet the cost demands of Surat’s weaving industry.
- Ahmedabad (The Cotton & Denim Hub): While domestic production of cotton apparel is vast, there is a substantial import market for synthetic sportswear, winter jackets (requiring specialized technical fabrics), and low-cost fast fashion.
- Industrial & Automotive Clusters (Sanand, Dahej): Consumes technical textiles, automotive seating fabrics, airbag materials, and filtration fabrics.
### 2.2 Local Market Demand
High demand for specialized yarns, including Draw Textured Yarn (DTY) for sarees and dress materials, Fully Drawn Yarn (FDY) for warp-knitting, and spandex-covered yarns for stretch fabrics.
### 2.3 Distribution Channels
- Broker / Agent Syndicates: Sourcing is highly mediated by brokers in Surat's Ring Road markets (e.g., Surat Textile Market, Millennium Market) who aggregate demand from small weavers and place bulk import orders.
- Direct B2B Sourcing: Large composite mills (e.g., Garden Silk Mills, Welspun, Arvind) import directly from Chinese/Vietnamese producers in containerized loads (FCL).
- Wholesale Agents: Traditional wholesale nodes in Ahmedabad's Revdi Bazar and Kalupur markets import containerized garment lots and distribute them to multi-brand retail outlets across Western India.

## 3. Comparative Analysis: China vs. Vietnam Sourcing Options
| Parameter | China Sourcing Option | Vietnam Sourcing Option |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Sourcing Variety & Flexibility | Complete vertical integration. Flexible MOQs (500-1,000 pcs), massive accessory markets (buttons, zippers, linings). | Highly competitive for long-run bulk orders. Higher MOQs (3,000+ pcs). Dependent on imported raw yarns/trims. |
| Bilateral Tariffs (India) | High Tariffs. Synthetic filament yarns: 10% - 12.5% BCD. Fabrics: 20% BCD (or specific duty). Readymade Garments (RMG): 20% - 25% BCD. Subject to BIS QCOs on yarns. | AIFTA Preferential Rates: Synthetic yarns, fabrics, and RMG at 0% - 5% BCD. Bypassing Chinese BCD saves 15% to 20% in direct tariffs. |
| Shipping Logistics | 15–22 days transit from Shanghai/Ningbo/Shenzhen to Mundra/Kandla. Freight (40HC): $1,800 - $2,600. | 10–14 days direct from Ho Chi Minh City to Mundra. Freight (20FT): $800 - $1,300. Vietnam shipping is 15-30% cheaper. |
| Regulatory Hurdles | BIS QCO for Polyester yarns (IS 17265, IS 17266), high customs duties. | CAROTAR 2020 value-addition checks, BIS QCO for Polyester yarns. |
| Feasibility Rating | Medium-Low (Saddled with 20-25% apparel BCD and yarn import tariffs) | Excellent (Saves ~20% BCD on RMG, cheaper freight, faster direct transit) |
