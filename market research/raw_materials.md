# Market Sourcing Report: Raw Materials
## 1. Supply-Side Analysis
### 1.1 China (The Global Commodity Giant)
China is the world's largest producer and exporter of structural metals, synthetic resins, and chemical intermediates.
- Major Manufacturing Hubs:
- Steel Coils (HRC/CRC): Hebei Province (Tangshan and Shijiazhuang - the steel heartland), Liaoning Province (Anshan), Shandong Province, and Jiangsu Province.
- Organic Cotton: Xinjiang Uyghur Autonomous Region (produces over 85% of China's cotton), with spinning mills located in Shandong, Henan, and Zhejiang.
- Chemical Intermediates & Resins: Shandong Province (Zibo, Dongying petrochemical clusters), Jiangsu Province (chemical industrial parks along the Yangtze River), and Zhejiang Province (Ningbo).
- Production Capacities:
China accounts for over 50% of global steel production (exceeding 1 billion metric tons annually) and is the world’s largest producer of chemical intermediates (including PVC, PTA, plasticizers, and organic compounds).
- Pricing Structure (FOB China):
- Steel Coils (HRC): Highly competitive, serving as a global benchmark.
- PVC Resins / Specialty Chemicals: Benefiting from highly integrated upstream coal-to-chemical and oil refining processes.
### 1.2 Vietnam (The Flexible Regional Alternative)
Vietnam has developed strong regional capacity in specific commodity segments, notably steel and synthetic yarn.
- Major Manufacturing Hubs:
- Steel Coils: Ha Tinh Province (Formosa Ha Tinh Steel), Quang Ngai Province (Hoa Phat Dung Quat Steel), and Ba Ria-Vung Tau Province.
- Organic Cotton / Yarn: Nam Dinh Province (Northern textile hub), Dong Nai Province, and Binh Duong Province.
- Chemical Intermediates: Phu My (petrochemical complex) and Nhon Trach industrial zones.
- Production Capacities:
- Steel: Vietnam is Southeast Asia's leading steel producer with over 20 million tons of capacity, exporting high volumes of HRC and galvanized coils.
- Cotton Yarn: Vietnam imports raw fiber (primarily from the US, India, and Australia) and spins it into high-grade organic and carded yarns for export.
- Chemicals: Relatively weak; relies on importing chemical feedstocks and focuses mainly on down-stream processing (plastic resins and compounds).
- Pricing Structure (FOB Vietnam):
Steel and yarn pricing is highly competitive with China, though specialized chemical pricing is 10% to 15% higher due to import-dependent supply lines.

## 2. Demand-Side Analysis (India, specifically Gujarat)
### 2.1 Import Volumes & Demand Drivers
Gujarat is the chemical and petrochemical capital of India. Massive demand is generated by:
- Petrochemical & Chemical Clusters: The Dahej PCPIR (Petroleum, Chemicals and Petrochemicals Investment Region) and industrial clusters in Ankleshwar, Vapi, Nandesari, and Hazira require enormous volumes of chemical intermediates and plastic resins (like PVC).
- Automobile Manufacturing: The automotive hub at Sanand and industrial fabrication units across the state consume huge quantities of hot-rolled and cold-rolled steel coils.
- Textile Processing: Surat and Ahmedabad are textile megahubs consuming organic cotton yarn and synthetic yarns for weaving and garment manufacturing.
### 2.2 Local Market Demand
High demand exists for PVC resin for pipe manufacturing, high-grade steel sheets for automotive stamping, and organic cotton yarn for export-quality fabrics.
### 2.3 Distribution Channels
- Direct Indenting / Bulk Consignments: Major industrial groups (e.g., Reliance, Adani, Welspun, Arvind) import raw materials directly in bulk vessels or containerized shipments through Mundra, Kandla, or Dahej ports.
- Importers / Stockists: Trade houses situated in Ahmedabad and Vadodara buy bulk steel and chemicals, store them in custom-bonded warehouses, and distribute them in smaller quantities to MSMEs.

## 3. Comparative Analysis: China vs. Vietnam Sourcing Options
| Parameter | China Sourcing Option | Vietnam Sourcing Option |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Capacity & Availability | Unmatched capacity and wide grade variety (specialty steels, thousands of chemical compounds). | High capacity in structural steel (HRC) and cotton yarn, but limited specialty chemical intermediates. |
| Tariffs & Trade Barriers | High risk. Basic Customs Duty: PVC Resin at 7.5%, Specialty Chemicals at 7.5%-10%, Steel Coils at 15%. India frequently imposes Anti-Dumping Duties (ADD) on Chinese PVC resin ($60 to $150 per MT) and flat steel. BIS QCOs are strictly enforced. | Low risk. Preferential AIFTA rates (0% to 5% BCD on PVC, PET, steel coils, and chemicals). Exempt from the Chinese ADDs, making Vietnam highly cost-effective for steel and plastic resins. |
| Sustainability & Traceability | Xinjiang cotton faces severe global compliance issues (UFLPA). Chemical plants face periodic environmental shutdowns. | Highly trace-compliant organic cotton yarn (processed from imported US/Australian fiber). More stable operations. |
| Shipping Logistics | High frequency of bulk/break-bulk vessels directly to Mundra/Kandla. Transit: 15–22 days. | Good containerized shipping to Mundra. Transit: 10–14 days. Break-bulk capacity for heavy steel is less frequent than China. |
| Regulatory Hurdles | Heavy ADD risks, strict BIS certifications. | CAROTAR 2020 value-addition checks, BIS certifications. |
| Feasibility Rating | Medium-Low (High tariff + ADD risk, QCO barriers) | High (Exempt from ADD, low tariffs for PVC/Yarn, fast shipping) |
